Russell’s Paradox

When I was first exposed to Set theory, I learnt about Russell’s paradox. To me it basically boils down to this. A set can be defined either by enumerating all the elements of a set or by stating a “set comprehension. A set comprehension is a rule or a property shared by all the elements of the set. Russell’s Paradox states that a comprehension with no restrictions leads to contradictions.

Electrons and Holes in Semiconductors

I found the explanation of hole and electron currents in semiconductors unsatisfactory until I learnt about valence electrons, energy bands etc.

Total current in a semiconductor is sum of electron current in conduction band and so called “hole current” in the valence band. Hole current is an abstraction of movement of electrons in the valence band. Electrons in the valence band are less mobile than the electrons in the conduction band. It is easier to think about movement of electrons in the valence band as the motion of “holes” or “absence of electrons” in the opposite direction.

∇ operator and Divergence

Divergence is represented as ∇.v i.e. a “dot product” of the ∇ operator and vector v. It is not actually a dot product because v.∇ makes no sense. It is just a notational convenience.
